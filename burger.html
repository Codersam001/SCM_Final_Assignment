Classic Homemade Burger

The perfect juicy burger recipe for your next cookout

Ingredients

For the Burger Patties:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (80% lean, 20% fat)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)

For the Burger Assembly:

  • 4 hamburger buns, lightly toasted
  • 4 slices cheese (American, cheddar, or your favorite)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Condiments: ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise

Instructions

  1. Prepare the patties: In a large bowl, gently mix ground beef with salt, pepper, Worcestershire sauce, and garlic powder (if using). Divide into 4 equal portions and form into patties about ¾-inch thick. Make a slight indentation in the center of each patty to prevent bulging during cooking.
  2. Cook the burgers: Preheat grill or skillet to medium-high heat. Cook patties for 3-4 minutes per side for medium (160°F internal temperature). Add cheese during the last minute of cooking if desired.
  3. Toast the buns: Lightly toast the cut sides of the buns on the grill or in a toaster.
  4. Assemble the burgers: Spread condiments on the bottom bun. Add lettuce, tomato, the cooked patty with cheese, onions, and pickles. Top with the other bun half.
  5. Serve immediately with your favorite sides like fries or coleslaw.

Chef's Tips:

  • For juicier burgers, don't overwork the meat when forming patties.
  • Let the cooked burgers rest for 2-3 minutes before serving.
  • Try different cheese varieties like blue cheese or pepper jack for variation.
  • Add a fried egg or bacon for a delicious twist.
