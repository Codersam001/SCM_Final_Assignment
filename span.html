DELICIOUS Spaghetti WITH TOMATO SAUCE

Ingredients

For the pasta:

  • 200g (7 oz) Spaghetti
  • Water (for boiling)
  • Salt (for the water)

For the Tomato Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 3-4 Garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 medium Onion, chopped
  • 1 can (400g/14oz) Crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp Tomato paste (optional)
  • 1 tsp Dried oregano (or 1 tbsp fresh)
  • 1 tsp Dried basil (or 1 tbsp fresh basil, chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground Black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley (for garnish, optional)
  • Parmesan cheese, freshly grated (optional)

Instructions:

1. Cook the Spaghetti:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions (8-10 minutes) until al dente.
  3. Drain the pasta, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water.

2. Make the Tomato Sauce:

  1. While the pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and chopped onion, saut√©ing until the onion is soft and translucent (about 4-5 minutes).
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste (if using), simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Add oregano, basil, and sugar (if using). Season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce cook for 5 more minutes.

3. Combine the Pasta and Sauce:

  1. Add the drained spaghetti to the pan with the tomato sauce.
  2. Toss the pasta in the sauce, adding some reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.

4. Serve:

Serve the spaghetti topped with fresh basil or parsley and a generous sprinkle of Parmesan cheese (optional). Enjoy!

Tips for Perfect Spaghetti:

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh basil, ripe tomatoes, and good-quality olive oil will elevate the flavors of your sauce.
  • Don't Overcook the Pasta: Make sure your spaghetti is cooked al dente (firm to the bite) for the best texture.
  • Add Salt to the Water: Salted pasta water is key to seasoning the pasta from the inside out.
  • Customize the Sauce: You can add extra flavor by including red pepper flakes, balsamic vinegar, or a splash of red wine to the sauce.
  • Make It Meatier: If you prefer a meat sauce, you can add ground beef, turkey, or sausage to the tomato sauce for added richness.
  • Leftovers: The tomato sauce can be stored in the fridge for up to 3 days or frozen for later use!
  • Try with Different Cheeses: If you don't have Parmesan, try Pecorino Romano, or for a creamier option, try adding some ricotta on top.
