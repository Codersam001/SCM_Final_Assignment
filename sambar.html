Sambar

Ingredients

For the Sambar Base:

  • Toor Dal
  • Water
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Vegetables
  • Salt
  • Tamarind
  • Sambar Powder

For the Tempering(Tadka)

  • Oil
  • Mustard Seeds
  • Cumin Seeds
  • Fenugreek Seeds
  • Curry leaves
  • Dry Red Chilies
  • Hing
  • Fresh Coriander leaves

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Toor Dal:

      Wash the toor dal thoroughly and pressure cook it with 2 1/2 cups of water and 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder for 3-4 whistles or until soft.
      Once done, mash the dal slightly with a spoon and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Tamarind:

      If using tamarind pulp, soak 1 tablespoon tamarind in warm water for 10-15 minutes and extract the juice.
      If using tamarind paste, dissolve 1 tablespoon in about 1/2 cup of water.

  3. Cook the Vegetables:

      In a separate pot, add the chopped vegetables (carrots, drumsticks, potatoes, or other choices).
      Add water (about 1 1/2 cups), a pinch of turmeric powder, and cook the vegetables until tender. You can pressure cook the vegetables with dal if you prefer.

  4. Combine the Dal and Vegetables:

      Once the vegetables are cooked, add the mashed toor dal to the pot of vegetables.
      Add the tamarind extract or tamarind paste and sambar powder.
      Bring the mixture to a gentle boil and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to allow all the flavors to meld together. If it's too thick, you can add a little more water to reach your desired consistency.
      Adjust salt and sambar powder to taste. (If you like it spicier, you can add a little more sambar powder.)

  5. Tempering (Tadka):

      In a small pan, heat 2 teaspoons of oil or ghee.
      Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
      Add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds (if using), dried red chilies, and curry leaves. Let it fry for about 30 seconds.
      Add asafoetida (hing), if using, and fry for a few seconds.
      Pour this tempering over the simmering sambar.

  6. Garnish and Serve:

      Stir the sambar well and let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes after the tempering is added.
      Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Tips for Perfect Dosa :

  • Consistency
  • Flavor Balance
  • Sambar Powder
